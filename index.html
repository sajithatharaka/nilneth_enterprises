Ceylon Cinnamon

Ceylon Cinnamon is known as PURE CINNAMON and is obtained from the bark of Cinnamonum zealanicum, which is an evergreen plant native to Sri Lanka (Ceylon). A major part of world production of pure cinnamon originates from Sri Lanka. The unique method of processing and curing cinnamon, brings about the characteristic flavor.

Pepper

A variety of wild pepper commonly called KING OF SPICES is native to Sri Lanka (Ceylon). Pepper comes from an evergreen climbing plant which grows up to 10m in height and is called Piper nigrum. Ceylon pepper is important in the world spice market, due to its high Piperine content, strong aroma and rich flavour. Pepper has two varieties named ‘Black Pepper’ and ‘White Pepper’.

Cloves

Cloves are a spice made from the flower buds of an evergreen tree called the clove tree (Syzygium aromaticum) which is of medium size and has a smooth bark. Clove flower buds are harvested in their immature state and then dried. Cloves can be used whole or ground, and they have a very strong, pungent flavor and aroma. This condiment also feature in desserts, especially ground cloves.

Black Pepper

Black Pepper is a perennial climbing vine plant, which is used for spices and medicines.

Nutmeg

Nutmeg is a spice made from the seed of the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans). It is the seed of the fruit, and mace is the dried net-like covering or aril of the seed. This spice which comes from mid country areas in Sri Lanka, is mostly used for production of essential oils, and other related commercial products.

White Pepper

This type of pepper is produced by the removal of the outer covering membrane. This can be done before or after the process of drying in order to obtain the inner seed.

Cinnamon Powder

Cinnamon powder, also known as ground cinnamon is made by grinding cinnamon sticks, chips, quillings and featherings. The quality of cinnamon powder depends on source. There is no other way of determining the quality of Cinnamon powder unless from laboratory tests. Sri Lanka exports Ceylon cinnamon as well as Ceylon cinnamon powder. Cinnamon powder is widely used in bakery products such as cinnamon rolls. By adding ground Cinnamon to Tea, Coffee, or any other food preparation, natural health benefits are acquired. By boiling a one table spoon cinnamon powder, for 5-10 minutes will result in making a drink which reduces cholesterol & diabetes.

    • Nutmeg

    Nutmeg is a spice made from the seed of the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans). It is the seed of the fruit, and mace is the dried net-like covering or aril of the seed. This spice which comes from mid country areas in Sri Lanka, is mostly used for production of essential oils, and other related commercial products. It is evergreen and grows to a maximum height of about 20m. The flowers are unisexual while the leaves are dark green and alternate. The fruits, fleshy and broad also have longitudinal ridges along their circumference. During ripening, the colour of fruits changes to yellow. Moreover, splitting of the pericarp into two halves also occurs thereby exposing the seeds. These seeds are shiny and are covered by red aril.
